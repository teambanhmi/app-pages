Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 15, 2026

Short version: Stillness collects nothing. No account, no analytics, no server. Everything you do in the app stays on your device.

What data we collect

Stillness collects no personal data. Specifically:

  • We do not collect your name, email, or any identifying information.
  • We do not require an account to use the app.
  • We do not use analytics SDKs (no Firebase, no Mixpanel, no Amplitude).
  • We do not embed advertising networks.
  • We do not track how you use the app.

Data stored on your device

Stillness stores the following data locally using Apple's SwiftData framework:

  • Session history: date, duration, session type (timer or breathing), sound chosen, breathing pattern, and breathing rounds completed.
  • Timer preferences: last-used duration, bell tone, interval setting, and sound selection.
  • App settings: streak display preference.

This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted anywhere.

Apple Health integration

If you grant permission, Stillness writes Mindful Minutes records to Apple Health after each session. This data goes directly from your device to Health — Stillness has no server involvement. You can revoke access at any time in Settings → Privacy → Health.

Stillness only writes to Health; it never reads your Health data.

Third-party services

Stillness uses no third-party services, SDKs, or frameworks that transmit data. The only external communication is:

  • App Store: Apple's standard purchase and update delivery infrastructure.
  • Apple Health: Only if you opt in, as described above.

Data deletion

To delete all session history, go to Settings → Delete All Sessions inside the app. To remove all data including preferences, delete the app from your device. Reinstalling starts completely fresh.

Children's privacy

Stillness does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. Since we collect no data at all, this is inherent to our design.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy in a meaningful way (e.g., if we add analytics), we will update the effective date and release a new app version. Continued use of the updated app constitutes acceptance.
